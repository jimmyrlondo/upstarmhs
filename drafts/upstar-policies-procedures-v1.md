## 1) Governance and Document Control

**Policy**  
Upstar Mental Health Services maintains a controlled policy system to ensure services are ethical, person-centered, safe, and operationally consistent.

**Procedures**
1. CEO approves all new and materially revised policies before release.
2. Policies are assigned an owner, version number, effective date, and next review date.
3. Policy owners complete annual review and submit updates when legal, regulatory, safety, payer, or operational changes occur.
4. Obsolete versions are archived and removed from active use.
5. Current approved policies are accessible to staff, contractors, and board members as applicable.

**Required Records**
- Policy register and version log
- Approval records (CEO)
- Annual policy review log

---

## 2) Leadership and Strategic Planning

**Policy**  
Leadership sets mission, direction, and strategic priorities that promote outcomes for persons served, financial stability, compliance, and continuous improvement.

**Procedures**
1. Leadership maintains and communicates mission, vision, and service commitments.
2. A strategic plan is developed with input from persons served, workforce, and stakeholders.
3. Strategic priorities include service quality, access, workforce capacity, technology, and risk mitigation.
4. Strategic plan progress is reviewed at least quarterly; full relevance review occurs annually.

**Required Records**
- Strategic plan and annual refresh
- Stakeholder input summaries
- Leadership/board review minutes

---

## 3) Legal and Regulatory Compliance Framework (Ohio)

**Policy**  
Upstar complies with applicable federal, Ohio, and local legal and regulatory requirements for behavioral health operations.

**Procedures**
1. Compliance tracking includes, at minimum: licensure/certification, privacy/confidentiality, records, employment, safety, billing, and telehealth.
2. Upstar maintains a compliance calendar with assigned accountable owners.
3. Suspected noncompliance is reported, triaged, corrected, and tracked to closure.
4. Policies requiring legal specificity are flagged: **[OHIO LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]**.
5. This manual is operational guidance and not legal advice.

**Required Records**
- Compliance inventory and calendar
- Corrective action log
- Compliance training attestations

## 4) Financial Stewardship and Internal Controls

**Policy**  
Financial practices support mission delivery, continuity of services, and responsible stewardship of funds.

**Procedures**
1. Annual operating budget is prepared before fiscal year start and approved by authorized leadership.
2. Budget assumptions consider historical trends, expected demand, payer environment, and staffing needs.
3. Monthly budget-to-actual review is completed; significant variances trigger corrective action.
4. Segregation of duties is maintained for authorization, custody, and reconciliation functions.
5. Independent financial review/audit is completed at least annually as required.

**Required Records**
- Approved annual budget
- Monthly financial variance reports
- Annual audit/review documentation

---

## 5) Risk Management and Safety

**Policy**  
Upstar uses a documented risk management process to reduce harm to persons served, workforce, contractors, property, and operations.

**Procedures**
1. Maintain a risk register with identified exposures, likelihood/severity, controls, and owners.
2. Review and update risk controls at least annually and after major incidents.
3. Maintain emergency and critical incident procedures for service and administrative locations.
4. Provide orientation and annual competency-based safety training, including incident reporting and emergency response.
5. Maintain and annually review insurance coverage for adequacy.

**Required Records**
- Risk register and annual review
- Incident reports and trend analyses
- Safety training records

---

## 6) Workforce Development and Training

**Policy**  
Upstar develops and manages a qualified workforce (employees and contractors) aligned with service quality, person-centered care, and safety.

**Procedures**
1. Define workforce categories, role requirements, and required credentials.
2. Perform verification of qualifications before independent service delivery.
3. Conduct orientation and ongoing training based on role and risk.
4. Complete documented supervision/performance review at defined intervals.
5. Maintain succession coverage for key leadership and clinical roles.
6. Provide cybersecurity and technology-use training relevant to job duties.

**Required Records**
- Personnel/contractor files
- Credential verification logs
- Training matrix and completion records

---

## 7) Rights of Persons Served

**Policy**  
Upstar protects and promotes each person served’s rights, dignity, privacy, and informed choice.

**Procedures**
1. Provide rights information in clear, understandable language at intake and as needed.
2. Protect persons served from abuse, neglect, exploitation, retaliation, and discrimination.
3. Obtain and document informed consent/refusal for services and release of information.
4. Maintain a grievance process with timely review, response, and appeal options.
5. Analyze formal complaints at least annually for trends and corrective actions.

**Required Records**
- Rights acknowledgment forms
- Grievance logs and resolutions
- Annual complaint trend analysis

---

## 8) Accessibility

**Policy**  
Upstar removes barriers to access for persons served, workforce, and stakeholders.

**Procedures**
1. Assess barriers in architecture, communication, technology, transportation, finances, and attitudes.
2. Provide language and communication accommodations as needed.
3. Integrate accessibility actions into planning and improvement work.
4. Track accommodation requests, response times, and unresolved barriers.
5. **[ENHANCEMENT]** Use plain-language and culturally responsive communication standards across all service materials, including materials targeted to approximately an 8th-grade reading level, interpreter/translation access when needed, and annual cultural-competency refresher expectations for relevant roles.

**Required Records**
- Accessibility assessment and action plan
- Accommodation request log
- Accessibility improvement updates

---

## 9) Performance Measurement and Improvement

**Policy**  
Upstar uses measurement and improvement cycles to strengthen outcomes, access, safety, and operational performance.

**Procedures**
1. Maintain a performance measurement plan covering service effectiveness, experience, access, efficiency, and business functions.
2. Collect baseline, interval, discharge, and follow-up data as appropriate.
3. Complete at least annual analysis of trends, comparators, and contributing factors.
4. Develop and implement corrective action plans for indicators below target.
5. Communicate relevant performance results to leadership and stakeholders.

**Required Records**
- Performance measurement plan
- KPI dashboard and annual analysis
- Improvement action plans and closure evidence

---

## 10) Program and Service Structure

**Policy**  
Each program has a clear scope, defined eligibility parameters, and resources needed for safe and effective service delivery.

**Procedures**
1. Document program scope, including populations, settings, hours, referral sources, payer/funding, and services offered.
2. Document information and communication technologies (ICT) modalities when used.
3. Define and publish entry, transition, and exit criteria.
4. Review and update program scope at least annually.
5. Ensure staffing model and operational resources match program commitments.

**Required Records**
- Program scope statements
- Entry/transition/exit criteria
- Annual scope review documentation

---

## 11) Screening and Access to Services

**Policy**  
Screening and access processes are timely, person-centered, and designed to connect persons served to appropriate care.

**Procedures**
1. Use written procedures for screening, eligibility, admission prioritization, and referral pathways.
2. Minimize elapsed time between first contact, screening, and admission/referral.
3. If urgent or critical needs are identified, take immediate action directly or via emergency referral.
4. If services are not appropriate/available, provide warm referral to alternate resources.
5. **[ENHANCEMENT]** Track access equity indicators (e.g., wait time by payer, geography, and language needs) with baseline measurement at implementation and at least quarterly reporting for continuous improvement.

**Required Records**
- Screening and admission procedures
- Wait-time and access reports
- Referral documentation

---

## 12) Person-Centered Planning

**Policy**  
Each person served has an individualized, culturally responsive, person-centered plan based on strengths, needs, abilities, and preferences.

**Procedures**
1. Engage the person served actively in plan development and updates.
2. Include family/supports and other providers when authorized and appropriate.
3. Set understandable, measurable, achievable, and time-specific goals.
4. Define services, interventions, frequency, and responsible team members.
5. Review plan progress at defined intervals and revise as clinically indicated.

**Required Records**
- Person-centered plans and updates
- Progress notes linked to goals
- Plan review records

---

## 13) Transition and Discharge

**Policy**  
Transition and discharge are planned to support continuity, recovery, and safety.

**Procedures**
1. Start transition planning as early as clinically appropriate.
2. Use written procedures for referral, transfer, discharge, and follow-up.
3. Coordinate with receiving providers and community supports.
4. Provide the person served a transition plan and relevant instructions.
5. Complete discharge summary for planned and unplanned discharge events.
6. **[ENHANCEMENT]** Attempt post-discharge follow-up contact within 7 calendar days for routine discharges and within 48 hours for high-risk discharges to support continuity and monitor early risk.

**Required Records**
- Transition plans
- Discharge summaries
- Follow-up outreach log

## 14) Medication Safety (When Applicable)

**Policy**  
When medication-related services are provided, Upstar ensures safe medication control, administration, prescribing coordination, and education.

**Procedures**
1. Apply medication procedures consistent with scope of service and applicable law.
2. Provide orientation and annual medication safety training for relevant direct service personnel.
3. Educate persons served (and authorized supports) on benefits, risks, side effects, adherence, and who to contact with concerns.
4. Maintain medication documentation, reconciliation processes, and escalation pathways for medication concerns.
5. **[OHIO LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]** Validate delegated medication tasks against Ohio scope-of-practice and facility/program rules.

**Required Records**
- Medication training records
- Medication education documentation
- Medication incident/variance logs

## 15) Nonviolent and De-escalation Practices

**Policy**  
Upstar prioritizes prevention and de-escalation; restrictive interventions are last-resort safety interventions only.

**Procedures**
1. Train personnel in trauma-informed de-escalation and crisis response.
2. Prohibit use of coercion, discipline, convenience, or retaliation as intervention rationale.
3. If restraint/seclusion is legally permitted within program scope, use only under defined emergency criteria with immediate release once risk resolves.
4. Conduct post-event review, debrief, and corrective action following each event.
5. Maintain a reduction plan with the goal of eliminating restrictive interventions where feasible.

**Required Records**
- Crisis/de-escalation training records
- Restrictive intervention event records (if applicable)
- Post-event review and trend analysis

---

## 16) Records, Quality Records, and Confidentiality

**Policy**  
Upstar maintains complete, current, secure records and uses record review to improve service quality.

**Procedures**
1. Maintain records that are organized, legible, complete, and timely.
2. Implement release-of-information procedures that meet legal requirements.
3. Limit access to authorized personnel and secure records through administrative, technical, and physical safeguards.
4. Define retention/destruction schedules and follow secure destruction methods.
5. Conduct documented quality record reviews at least quarterly using trained reviewers.
6. Use record-review findings for training, supervision, and quality improvement actions.

**Required Records**
- Records policy and ROI forms
- Access and disclosure logs
- Quarterly quality record review summaries

---

## 17) Telehealth / ICT Service Delivery Safeguards

**Policy**  
When services are delivered via telehealth/ICT, quality, privacy, safety, and identity controls are maintained at standards comparable to in-person care.

**Procedures**
1. Obtain and document consent specific to ICT service delivery.
2. Verify provider identity, person served identity, and person served physical location at each encounter.
3. Confirm technology functionality and privacy conditions before and during services.
4. Maintain contingency procedures for technology disruption and urgent safety response.
5. Reassess appropriateness of ICT modality throughout care.
6. **[ENHANCEMENT]** Require a documented backup communication method (e.g., phone) before each ICT session, recorded in the encounter note, designated telehealth workflow field, or (if no EHR field exists) a standardized telehealth session checklist retained with quality records.

**Required Records**
- ICT consent documentation
- Encounter-level identity/location verification notes
- ICT disruption and contingency logs

---

## 18) Governance Oversight and Reporting

**Policy**  
Leadership and board-level oversight (where applicable) receive regular reports on compliance, quality, safety, and strategic progress.

**Procedures**
1. Leadership reviews key dashboards at least quarterly.
2. Governance bodies receive periodic reports on risk, finance, quality, and rights/grievances.
3. Significant incidents and corrective actions are escalated per policy.
4. Annual management review confirms policy relevance and sets priorities for the next cycle.

**Required Records**
- Leadership review packets
- Governance meeting records
- Annual management review summary
